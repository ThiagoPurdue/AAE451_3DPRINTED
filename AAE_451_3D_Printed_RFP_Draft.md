# AAE 451 Request for Proposals (RFP): 3D-Printed Fixed-Wing Aircraft Design, Build, and Flight Test

## Project Objectives

We seek proposals from student teams (up to 10 teams) to design, build, and flight test a remotely piloted, propeller-driven, fixed-wing aircraft where the primary airframe is manufactured using 3D printing technologies. The primary objective is to create an aircraft that can take off from a runway, safely complete a specified flight circuit at the designated airfield, and perform a safe landing, demonstrating the viability, modularity, and structural integrity of 3D-printed aerospace structures.

## Project Requirements

The project requirements are as follows:

**Airframe and Manufacturing Limitations:**

* **3D Printing Requirement:** The primary structural components of the aircraft (fuselage, wings, and empennage) must be manufactured using Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) 3D printing. Acceptable materials include PLA, PETG, ABS, ASA, or Lightweight (LW) PLA.
* **Printer Volume Constraint:** Individual printed components must be designed to fit within a standard desktop 3D printer build volume (e.g., 250 mm x 250 mm x 300 mm). This forces teams to consider joining methods.
* **Structural Reinforcement:** The use of off-the-shelf carbon fiber or aluminum tubes/rods as wing spars or tail booms is permitted and encouraged for structural integrity.

**Size and Weight Constraints:**

* **Maximum Weight:** The aircraft Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW) cannot exceed 3 kg (29.43 N).
* **Maximum Wingspan:** The aircraft wingspan cannot exceed 1.5 meters (150 cm).
* **Storage Limitation:** The aircraft must be stored within a 50 cm width x 50 cm height x 100 cm length storage volume when not actively being flown or worked on. Because the wingspan is larger than the storage length, the design must be modular and easily assembled/disassembled.

**Flight Performance:**

* **Takeoff:** The aircraft must take off (all ground contact points off the ground) within 20 meters of the start line.
* **Mission Profile:** After takeoff, the aircraft will climb to a safe operating altitude (e.g., 30 m) and complete 3 laps around the designated flight circuit.
* **Landing:** The aircraft must be capable of performing a soft field landing (packed dirt base, potentially wet, with grass) without sustaining structural damage to the 3D-printed components.
* **Power:** The complete propulsion system must not consume more than 800 W of electrical power.

**Operational and Safety:**

* **Assembly Time:** The aircraft must transition from the storage configuration to the flight configuration (minus battery and payload) in less than 5 minutes.
* **Battery Safety:** The battery must be safely connected without any appendages crossing the arc of the propeller and without removing major aircraft components.
* **Payload:** The aircraft must be designed to carry a payload of either 500 g or 1 kg securely within the fuselage. Teams must demonstrate stable flight with both payload configurations.

## Provided Avionics and Data Collection

Each team will be provided with the following control, avionic, and sensor components:

* 2.4 GHz transmitter and receiver
* Pixhawk flight controller (e.g., Pixhawk 6C mini) and included cables
* Power module for Pixhawk
* GPS antenna
* Digital airspeed sensor

Teams will be responsible for incorporating the provided electronics into their aircraft design and utilizing them for aircraft control and collecting the following parameters during flight testing:

* GPS altitude, groundspeed, climb rate, and distance flown
* Airspeed
* Aircraft acceleration, load factor, turn rate, and roll rate

## Materials and Budget

In addition to the provided electronics, each team will have a budget of $300 to purchase materials and items specific to their aircraft.

* Materials sourced from the lab, such as hardware, 3D printing filament, motors, ESCs, propellers, control linkages, and servos, shall be included in the budget.
* **Material Tracking:** Teams must accurately log their 3D printing material usage (by weight of filament consumed, including support material and failed prints) and factor this into their budget to encourage efficient design and minimize waste.
* Teams must order off-the-shelf components from a list of approved suppliers. Any deviations must be formally requested in writing to the teaching team.

## Evaluation

Aircraft will be evaluated on their ability to complete the outlined mission while meeting all specified requirements, with a particular emphasis on the structural efficiency, printability, and modularity of the 3D-printed design.
